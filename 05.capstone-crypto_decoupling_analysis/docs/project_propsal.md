# Project Proposal

## Project Title

**Has Cryptocurrency Decoupled from Traditional Financial Markets?
Evidence from Bitcoin, Ethereum, Gold, and the S&P 500 (2019–2024)**

---

#  Introduction

Cryptocurrencies have evolved from niche digital assets into globally traded financial instruments. Bitcoin and Ethereum, in particular, have attracted significant attention from retail and institutional investors due to their rapid growth and potential role in portfolio diversification.

Traditionally, assets such as Gold have been viewed as safe-haven investments during periods of economic uncertainty, while equity indices like the S&P 500 represent broader market performance. As cryptocurrencies become increasingly integrated into the global financial system, it is important to understand whether they continue to behave independently or have become closely linked with traditional financial markets.

This project applies Python-based data analytics techniques to investigate the changing relationships between cryptocurrencies and traditional assets over the period from 2019 to 2024.

---

#  Background

Bitcoin was originally introduced as a decentralized digital currency that could operate independently of governments and financial institutions. Over time, many investors began referring to Bitcoin as "Digital Gold" because of its perceived ability to preserve value during periods of market uncertainty.

However, recent events—including the COVID-19 pandemic, the 2021 cryptocurrency bull market, and the 2022 global market downturn—suggest that cryptocurrencies may increasingly move alongside equity markets rather than acting as independent hedge assets.

Understanding these relationships is valuable for investors, portfolio managers, and financial analysts seeking effective diversification strategies.

---

#  Business Problem

Investors rely on diversification to reduce portfolio risk. If cryptocurrencies are highly correlated with traditional financial assets, their ability to provide diversification benefits may be limited.

This project aims to evaluate whether Bitcoin and Ethereum have become more closely associated with traditional financial markets or continue to behave as independent investment assets.

---

#  Research Question

> **Has Cryptocurrency Decoupled from Traditional Financial Markets? Evidence from Bitcoin, Ethereum, Gold, and the S&P 500 (2019–2024).**

---

#  Project Objectives

## Primary Objective

Evaluate whether Bitcoin and Ethereum have become more independent from traditional financial markets between 2019 and 2024.

## Secondary Objectives

* Compare historical price movements across all four assets.
* Analyze daily and cumulative returns.
* Measure rolling correlations between cryptocurrencies and traditional assets.
* Compare volatility across different market conditions.
* Evaluate maximum drawdowns during major market events.
* Measure risk-adjusted performance using Sharpe Ratio.
* Develop a custom **Crypto Decoupling Index (CDI)**.
* Provide evidence-based investment insights.

---

#  Research Hypotheses

## Hypothesis 1 – Market Correlation

**Null Hypothesis (H₀):**

Bitcoin and Ethereum remain significantly correlated with traditional financial markets.

**Alternative Hypothesis (H₁):**

Bitcoin and Ethereum have become less correlated with traditional financial markets over time.

---

## Hypothesis 2 – Risk Behaviour

**Null Hypothesis (H₀):**

Cryptocurrencies exhibit risk characteristics similar to traditional financial assets.

**Alternative Hypothesis (H₁):**

Cryptocurrencies exhibit significantly higher volatility and drawdowns than traditional financial assets.

---

## Hypothesis 3 – Investment Performance

**Null Hypothesis (H₀):**

Cryptocurrencies do not provide superior risk-adjusted returns compared to traditional assets.

**Alternative Hypothesis (H₁):**

Cryptocurrencies provide superior long-term returns despite higher risk.


        Hypothesis 1 → Correlation
        Hypothesis 2 → Risk
        Hypothesis 3 → Performance

#  Limitations

* The analysis is based on historical market data and does not predict future prices.
* Correlation measures association rather than causation.
* Macroeconomic and geopolitical events may influence asset prices but are not modeled explicitly.
* The study focuses on four representative assets and does not include the broader cryptocurrency market.
