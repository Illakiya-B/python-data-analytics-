# Has Cryptocurrency Decoupled from Traditional Financial Markets?

### *Evidence from Bitcoin, Ethereum, Gold, and the S&P 500 (2019–2024)*

##  Project Overview

Cryptocurrencies were once promoted as assets that could diversify investment portfolios and act as a hedge against traditional financial markets. However, major events such as the COVID-19 pandemic and the 2022 crypto market crash have raised an important question:

> **Has cryptocurrency become independent from traditional financial markets, or does it behave like a traditional risk asset?**

This project aims to answer that question through a comprehensive data analytics workflow using Python. By comparing Bitcoin (BTC), Ethereum (ETH), Gold (GLD), and the S&P 500 Index, this study investigates changes in market relationships, volatility, and investment behavior between **2019 and 2024**.

---

##  Research Question

> **Has Cryptocurrency Decoupled from Traditional Financial Markets? Evidence from Bitcoin, Ethereum, Gold, and the S&P 500 (2019–2024).**

---

##  Project Objectives

* Compare the performance of Bitcoin, Ethereum, Gold, and the S&P 500.
* Analyze daily returns and cumulative growth.
* Measure rolling correlations to evaluate market decoupling.
* Compare volatility and drawdowns across different market conditions.
* Evaluate risk-adjusted performance using financial metrics.
* Develop a **Crypto Decoupling Index (CDI)** as a custom analytical metric.
* Provide evidence-based investment insights and business recommendations.
